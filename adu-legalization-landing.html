FAQ

Questions homeowners ask first.

What if my basement can't be legalized at all?

Most can, with specific fixes — ceiling height, egress windows, fire separation. On the rare file where it genuinely can't, I tell you on the free call, before any paid work, and walk you through what rebuilding or other options would cost instead.

How much does this actually cost?

It depends on your scope — existing layout, what the city is asking for, and whether this is a fresh application or a rescue. You'll get an exact number in writing after the free feasibility call, no surprise add-ons.

How long will this take from here to a stamped permit?

Drawings typically take 2–4 weeks once I've assessed your space. City review timelines vary by municipality, but well-prepared files average far fewer examiner rounds than DIY submissions — which is where most of the delay usually comes from.

The city already rejected my application — can you fix it?

Yes — this is the Stuck Application Rescue service. Send me the examiner's comments and I'll tell you exactly what's missing, then produce revised drawings that answer every point in one resubmission instead of three.
